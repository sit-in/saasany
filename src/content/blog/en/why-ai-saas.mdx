## The Moment We Are In

The cost of intelligence has dropped to near zero. Models that would have required a research team and millions of dollars to run two years ago are now available via an API call for fractions of a cent. This is not a small shift — it is the kind of structural change that rewrites entire industries, and the developers who move first will capture the most value.

AI SaaS sits at the intersection of two durable trends: the software-as-a-service business model, which provides recurring revenue and compounding growth, and artificial intelligence, which enables products that genuinely solve hard problems. The combination is unusually powerful.

## What Makes AI SaaS Different

Traditional software automates repetitive, rule-based tasks. AI software handles tasks that were previously impossible to automate — understanding intent, generating creative output, reasoning across unstructured data, and adapting to context. This means you can build products that replace or augment knowledge work rather than just clerical work.

The competitive moat is also different. With conventional software, competitors can copy features. With AI products, the moat comes from proprietary data, fine-tuned models, carefully designed workflows, and the trust of users who have trained the product on their specific needs. These advantages compound over time in ways that are hard to replicate.

## Where to Start

The highest-leverage starting point is a vertical AI product — software that does one thing exceptionally well for a specific industry or workflow. Horizontal AI assistants are dominated by well-funded incumbents. Vertical products can win by depth: understanding a domain better than a general-purpose tool, integrating with the specific data sources that matter, and delivering output that is immediately useful without prompting expertise.

Pick a problem you understand, build an MVP in a weekend using a template like Sassany, get it in front of real users, and iterate on feedback. The market is moving fast enough that a focused team shipping consistently will beat a large team planning carefully.
